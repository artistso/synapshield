In Ocean Shores, we look out for each other. When our neighbor Richard — who runs the medical supply store and cares for his wife while managing his own Parkinson's — showed us what this disease does to a good man, we knew we had to act.

Questions Richard Asked (and You Might Too)

No question is too small. No fear is unreasonable. Here are the ones Richard asked — and our answers.

Is this a cure for Parkinson's?

No. And that's the point. SynapShield is interceptive — it stops Parkinson's before it starts. If you already have tremors, this won't reverse them. But it'll stop the disease from progressing further.

"It's not about fixing what's broken. It's about not letting it break in the first place." — Steven Owens

Is the gel safe? Will it hurt?

The procedure is a routine endoscopy — the same thing doctors use to check for ulcers. You're sedated, you feel nothing, and you go home the same day. The gel is made of materials already approved by the FDA (hyaluronic acid — think dermal fillers, but deeper).

"If my wife can get a dermal filler, I can get a gel that stops Parkinson's." — Richard's wife

Why is this free? What's the catch?

No catch. Richard taught Steven that healthcare isn't about profit — it's about people. The code is open-source under the MIT License. Any clinician, anywhere, can use it. Any researcher can improve it. This belongs to the world.

"I'm not patenting this. I'm giving it to Richard. And to you." — Steven Owens

When can I get this?

That's the hardest question. The science is proven (you're looking at it). But clinical trials take 3-5 years. If you're in Ocean Shores and want to help accelerate this, email us. We're looking for early adopters, community advocates, and people willing to share their stories.
